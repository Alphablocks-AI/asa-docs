---
title: "See Asa Working"
description: "Your shoppers are talking to Asa. Here is how to read what's happening and know it's working."
---

Once Asa is live, your job shifts from setup to observation. This guide shows you what to look at, what it means, and what to do with it.

<Steps titleSize="h4">

<Step icon="inbox" title="Review Conversations">

Navigate to the **Activity > Conversations** section in the sidebar. Every shopper conversation Asa has had is here.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/all-conversations-view.png" alt="Conversations inbox showing list of conversations with status indicators" />
</Frame>

Three tabs across the top allow you to filter conversations by type for quick access:

| Tab | What it shows |
|---|---|
| All Conversations | Every conversation Asa has had |
| Assisted | Conversations Asa resolved without any human intervention |
| Redirected | Conversations handed over to your team either smartly or requested to by the shopper |

Each conversation shows a title, a one-line preview of what the shopper said or what Asa did, and how long ago it happened. The icon on the right tells you the status of the conversation as outlined below.

| Status | What it shows |
|---|---|
| All Conversations | Ongoing Conversation - Conversation that has been initiated but not yet tagged to be assisted or redirected |
| Assisted | Conversations Asa resolved without any human intervention |
| Redirected | Conversations handed over to your team either smartly or requested to by the shopper |

</Step>

<Step title="Read a conversation">

Click any conversation to open it. This is where you see exactly what happened between Asa and a real shopper on your store.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/conversation-detail.png" alt="Conversation detail showing shopper message, Asa response with product cards and shopper behavior events" />
</Frame>

Four things to pay attention to:

**The conversation itself**
Read Asa's response. Does it sound like your brand? Is the answer accurate? Is the product recommendation relevant to what the shopper actually asked?

**Shopper behavior events**
Below the conversation you'll see a behavioral trail — what the shopper did on your storefront during and after the conversation. Viewed a product page. Added to cart. Removed from cart. Cart nudge shown. This context tells you whether Asa's response actually influenced behavior, not just whether the shopper replied.

**Good and Sources**
At the bottom of Asa's response: **Good** marks the response as a quality example. **Sources** shows exactly which pieces of trained content Asa pulled from to generate the answer. If the answer was wrong, Sources tells you whether it was a knowledge gap or a training quality issue.

**Add Guidance and Add Snippet**
Top right of the conversation view. If you spot something Asa got wrong or could handle better, add a Guidance instruction or a Snippet of content directly from the conversation — without going back to Configuration. Fix the gap where you find it.

</Step>

<Step title="Check a shopper's full activity">

Click **User Activity** in the top right of any conversation. A panel opens with the full picture of that shopper across all their interactions with your store and Asa.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/user-activity.png" alt="User Activity panel showing shopper profile, storefront events and conversation history" />
</Frame>

Three sections:

**Profile**
Name, email, and location if available. Useful for identifying returning customers or high-value shoppers who had a poor experience.

**Storefront Events**
What the shopper did on your store during the selected period. Asa sees this context in real time during conversations — it's what enables Asa to engage at the right moment with the right message.

**Conversations**
All previous conversations this shopper has had with Asa. A shopper who had a redirected conversation two months ago and is back today is worth looking at.

</Step>

<Step title="Know what you're looking for">

The inbox is not a log. It's a diagnostic tool. Here is what to look for in your first week:

**Redirected conversations with the same topic**
If multiple shoppers got handed over for the same question — returns, a specific product, an ingredient — that's a gap in your knowledge base. Add that content and retrain.

**Responses that don't sound like your brand**
Go to **Configuration > Responses > Guidance** and tighten your Communication Style instructions.

**Shoppers who rephrased the same question**
A shopper who asks the same thing three different ways before getting an answer is telling you Asa's first response wasn't good enough. Check Sources on that conversation — the knowledge may exist but is poorly structured.

**Assisted conversations where the shopper bought**
This is the signal you want more of. Behavior events showing Add to Cart after an Asa conversation is Asa doing its job. Note what kind of question it was — product recommendation, ingredient query, size guidance — and make sure you have strong knowledge coverage in that area.

<Tip>
Spend 10 minutes in the inbox every day for your first two weeks. You will learn more about how Asa is performing — and what your shoppers actually need — than from any report.
</Tip>

</Step>

</Steps>
