---
title: "Make it your own"
description: "Asa works out of the box. This guide is to makes it yours. Matching your brand's look, voice, and behavior."
---

Shoppers form an impression of your brand in seconds. Every detail of how Asa looks and responds either reinforces that impression or breaks it. This guide walks you through getting both right.

<Steps titleSize="h4">
  <Step icon="palette" title="Set Appearance">
    Navigate to the **Configuration > Appearance** section in the sidebar.

    <Frame>
        <img src="/images/customize-appearance.png" alt="Appearance settings showing avatar, theme, widget position, brand color and assistant name fields" />
    </Frame>

    | Setting | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Avatar** | Upload your brand logo or a recognizable icon. This appears in the chat header and as the widget button on your storefront. |
    | **Theme** | Match your storefront, not your personal preference. If your store is dark, use dark. Consistency removes friction. |
    | **Widget Position** | Bottom right works for most stores. Move it to bottom left only if a fixed storefront element — a cart button, a sticky bar — conflicts with it. |
    | **Brand Color** | Your primary brand HEX. This fills the message bubbles and highlights in the widget. Most merchants use the exact same primary color as their storefront CTA buttons. |
    | **Assistant Name** | How Asa introduces itself to your shoppers. What works by brand type. For example, *Ask Nike*, *Zara AI*, and so on. |
    | **Input Placeholder** | Customize chat input placeholder text for when starting a new conversation set — it with intention. For example, *Find your fit...*, *Ask about ingredients, routines, or products...*, and so on.  |

    <Note>
    Use the preview panel on the right to check your changes before saving. Click **Save Changes** in the top right to apply.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step icon="sliders" title="Configure your responses">
    Navigate to the **Configuration > Responses** section in the sidebar.

    <Tabs>
    <Tab title="Behaviour">

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/customize-responses.png" alt="Responses settings showing tone of voice, response length and human support contact fields" />
    </Frame>

    | Setting | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Response Length** | How concise or detailed Asa's answers are. Use the preview panel on the right to preview your changes. |
    | **Tone of Voice** | Pick the tone that fits your brand personality. If you're unsure, read three of your best product descriptions or your blog posts out loud. The tone you hear is the one to pick. |
    | **Default Language** | Set this to your primary customer language. Asa adapts to the shopper's language in conversation regardless — this is just the default. |
    | **Human Support Contact** | Define how shoppers reach your team when Asa can't or shouldn't help. Set this before you go live. These conversations are tagged as **redirected** in conversations section. |
    | **Proactive Messages** | When enabled, Asa surfaces helpful prompts as shoppers browse — even before they ask a question. High-intent moments where it works well: product pages, the cart activities, and search. |

    </Tab>

    <Tab title="Guidance">

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/customize-responses-guidance.png" alt="Responses settings showing tone of voice, response length and human support contact fields" />
    </Frame>

    Guidance is where Asa gets your actual brand voice. Not a dropdown — instructions written by you, in plain language. Think of it as the brief you'd give a new team member on their first day.

    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Communication Style">
        Set the tone, vocabulary, and terminology Asa uses. Be specific. Vague instructions produce vague results.

        | Brand type | What to write |
        |---|---|
        | Fashion and apparel | "Use aspirational but accessible language. Refer to products by their full name. Never use slang. Always suggest complementary pieces when making a recommendation." |
        | Health and wellness | "Use warm, science-informed language. Avoid making medical claims. Always recommend consulting a healthcare professional for medical conditions. Use ingredient names accurately." |
        | Skincare and personal care | "Use clean, ingredient-forward language. Acknowledge skin concerns with empathy before recommending. Never guarantee results — use 'may help' or 'known to support'." |
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Context and Clarification">
        Tell Asa when to ask a follow-up question before answering. This is where you enable an already powerful shopping assistant into a brand-representing intelligence.

        Good examples:
        - "When a shopper asks for a routine recommendation, ask their skin type and primary concern first."
        - "When a shopper asks for a size recommendation, ask their usual size and the brand they normally wear."
        - "When a shopper asks about supplements, ask about their health goal before recommending."
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Handover to Team">
        Define the lines Asa should never cross alone. Be explicit — Asa follows these precisely.

        Good examples:
        - "Hand over when a shopper requests a refund or mentions a damaged product."
        - "Escalate if the issue involves an account, payment, or order that cannot be found."
        - "Always hand over complaints — never attempt to resolve dissatisfaction without a human."
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Additional Guidelines">
        Anything that doesn't fit above. Common uses:
        - Topics Asa should never discuss: "Do not discuss competitor products."
        - Legal disclaimers: "Always add 'Results may vary' when discussing outcomes."
        - Products to prioritize or avoid in recommendations.
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>

    <Tip>
    Each Guidance field has a 1000 character limit. Be specific, not exhaustive. One clear instruction beats three vague ones.
    </Tip>
    </Tab>
    </Tabs>
    <Note>
    Use the preview panel on the right to check your changes before saving. Click **Save Changes** in the top right to apply.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
