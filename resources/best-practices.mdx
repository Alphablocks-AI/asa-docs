description: "Practical ways to keep Asa accurate: what to train, how to maintain content, and how to learn from shopper conversations."

Asa answers from what you train in the [dashboard](https://app.asa.so). Strong content beats volume — clear, current, non-conflicting sources produce the most trustworthy storefront answers.

<Steps titleSize="h4">

<Step icon="seedling" title="Start with what shoppers ask most">

Train the sources that resolve real questions first.

1. Go to **Add Content** in the sidebar.
2. Connect **Shopify** if you sell there — products, policies, and key pages cover most pre-purchase and support questions.
3. Add policies, sizing guides, or FAQs via **Files**, **Websites**, or **Notion** / **Google Drive** if they live outside Shopify.

<Check>
In **Manage Content**, every item you need at launch shows **Trained**. Use **View Content** on a row to confirm Asa extracted what you expect.
</Check>

</Step>

<Step icon="arrows-rotate" title="Keep content current">

| Practice | Why it helps |
|---|---|
| **Use integrations over one-off uploads** | Shopify, Notion, and Google Drive stay closer to your live catalog and docs. Shopify updates retrain the same source — they do not inflate your count with duplicates. |
| **Remove outdated trained sources** | Delete retired products, old policies, or broken URLs from **Manage Content** so Asa cannot cite stale answers. |
| **Retrain after major edits** | Changed a Notion page or re-uploaded a file? Retrain from **Manage Content** so the latest version is what shoppers get. |

<Warning>
Conflicting facts across sources (two different return windows, mismatched shipping copy) can confuse answers. Pick one canonical source per topic and remove or fix duplicates.
</Warning>

</Step>

<Step icon="comments" title="Learn from conversations">

Use **Activity > Conversations** to read what shoppers actually ask and where Asa escalated to your team (**redirected** conversations).

| Signal | What to do |
|---|---|
| Same question many times | Add or improve a trained source (product copy, policy page, Notion FAQ). |
| Wrong or thin answer | Open **View Content** on the relevant source; fix the source material, then retrain. |
| Team handles the same macro repeatedly | Turn the answer into a **Snippet** under **Add Content > Snippet** — snippets do not use plan knowledge source quota. |

On **Starter** and **Growth**, **Activity > Analytics** helps you spot volume and themes over time as traffic grows.

</Step>

<Step icon="scale-balanced" title="Prioritize quality over quantity">

More files do not always mean better support.

- Train pages shoppers reference — shipping, returns, sizing, ingredients, compatibility — not internal drafts.
- Write in plain language; Asa passes through structure and facts from your sources.
- Stay within your [knowledge source limit](/resources/trained-source-count). If you hit the cap, remove low-value trained rows or upgrade on [Plans and pricing](/resources/plan-pricing).

<Tip>
**Snippets** are ideal for short, stable answers (warranty one-liner, promo rules). Publish trained snippets as widget FAQs from **Manage Content** when you want them on the greeting screen.
</Tip>

</Step>

<Step icon="wand-magic-sparkles" title="Tune behavior after content is solid">

Once training looks good, align how Asa speaks and when it hands off:

- **Configuration > Appearance** — brand look and widget placement ([Make Asa your own](/get-started/make-asa-your-own)).
- **Configuration > Responses** — tone, length, human support contact, and **Guidance** instructions.
- **Configuration > Responses > Handover** — when to route to Gorgias or your team ([Gorgias integration](/integrations/gorgias)).

Content quality and behavior settings work together; fix training gaps before chasing tone tweaks alone.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Quick reference

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Connect Shopify and train high-intent catalog + policies | Training every product when only a subset is in stock or on sale |
| Delete unused sources in **Manage Content** | Leaving conflicting return or shipping copy in multiple files |
| Use **Snippet** for repeatable short answers | Uploading the same FAQ as PDF, URL, and Notion page |
| Review **Conversations** weekly after launch | Ignoring redirected threads that reveal missing policies |
