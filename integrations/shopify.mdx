Use one of the following setups to connect Asa to your Shopify account. The **Shopify App Setup** is the recommended path for most stores, while **Shopify Legacy Setup** is available for older configurations.

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Shopify App Setup">

Use this path if you want the simplest, most up-to-date way to connect Asa to your Shopify store using the official Asa app from the Shopify App Store.

### **Step 1: Install the Asa app from Shopify App Store**

- In your Shopify admin, go to the **Shopify App Store**.
- Search for **"Asa"**.
- Open the Asa app listing and click **Install**.
- Choose the Shopify store where you want to install Asa (if you manage multiple stores).

### **Step 2: Approve app permissions**

- Shopify will show you a permissions screen describing what the Asa app can access.
- Review the permissions carefully (products, content, orders, etc.).
- Click **Install app** to approve and continue.

Once approved, Shopify will automatically redirect you to the Asa Dashboard.

### **Step 3: Sign up or log in to Asa**

- If you already have an Asa account, **log in** with your existing credentials.
- If you’re new to Asa, **sign up** to create an account.
- After sign-in, you’ll be taken straight to the Shopify integration flow for the store you just connected.

### **Step 4: Complete the integration form**

- The Shopify integration form will open automatically.
- You’ll see two fields:
  - **Shopify Subdomain:** Your store subdomain (for example, `acme` if your admin URL is `https://admin.shopify.com/store/acme/...`).
  - **Shopify Online Store URL:** Your public storefront URL (for example, `https://acme.com`).
- Check that both values are correct and double-check for typos before continuing.

### **Step 5: Connect and wait for confirmation**

- When you’re ready, click **Proceed** or **Connect** to start the connection.
- The integration typically completes within **1–2 minutes**.
- Once connected, you’ll see a success state in the Asa Dashboard and the app will appear as **Connected** for that store.

After the app is connected, you can move on to selecting which products, pages, and policies you want Asa to learn.

<Tab title="Shopify Legacy Setup">

Follow these steps to connect Asa to your Shopify account using a **custom app** with limited Admin API access. This setup lets Asa learn from your product listings, metafields, blogs, pages, and store policies.

### **Step 1: Create a Custom App in Shopify**

- In your Shopify dashboard, go to **Settings** \> **Apps and Sales Channels** \> **Develop Apps** \> **Build apps in Dev Dashboard**.
- Click **Create App** from the top right.

[[****_______If you can’t access the Dev Dashboard section, you may not have the required setup/permissions. Check Shopify’s guide or contact your store admin. If you’re still having trouble, feel free to reach out to us at help@alphablocks.ai\*\*._______****](mailto:help@alphablocks.ai)](https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/apps/app-types/custom-apps)\*\*

### **Step 2: Name Your App**

- Name your app **"Asa Custom App"**
- Click **Create**

### **Step 3: Add Admin API Access Scopes**

Under the section **Access** \> **Scopes** copy-paste the following scopes:

```markdown Access Scopes
read_customers, read_discounts, read_fulfillments, read_inventory, read_legal_policies, read_metaobject_definitions, read_metaobjects, read_online_store_pages, read_orders, read_product_listings, read_products, read_publications, read_content, read_app_proxy, write_app_proxy, read_customer_events, read_themes, read_pixels, write_pixels
```

- Click **Release**
- **Click "Install" and select store to install it on**

### **Step 3: Navigate to Settings Tab**

- Go to the **Settings** tab
- Under Credentials, make note of **Client ID** and **Secret**. You'll need these in Asa Dashboard.

### **Step 4: Connect Shopify in Asa**

- In your **Asa dashboard**, go to **Add Content**, open the **Integrations** tab, and click **Connect** under **Shopify**.
- You’ll be asked to enter the following details:
  - **Admin API Access Client ID**:
  - Paste the lient ID you noted from previous step.
  - **Admin API Access Secret**:
  - Paste the Secret you noted from previous step.
  - **Storefront Public URL**:
  - This is your live store URL — for example:

    `https://example.com` or `https://acme.com`.
  - **Shopify Subdomain**:
  - In Shopify, go to **Settings \> Domains** and find the domain that ends in `.myshopify.com`.

    If your admin URL is:

    `https://admin.shopify.com/store/acme/settings/domains`

    Then your subdomain is: `acme`.

**___Make sure all fields are filled in accurately before clicking Connect to complete the setup.___**

### **Step 5: Select Content and Train**

- After connecting, a dialog will open to select products, blogs, pages, or policies
- If it doesn’t appear, click the  icon next to **Connected** under Shopify
- Choose the content you want Asa to learn from and click **Add Content**

Training usually takes a couple of minutes.

*To verify everything is working, go to **Manage Content** \> **Integrations** and check the training status of the selected items.*

- For secure setup, only the scopes you selected are accessed by Asa — nothing more
